What is a QMS?

A Quality Management System (QMS) is a structured framework of processes and procedures designed to ensure the quality, safety, and effectiveness of products in the life sciences sector. At Labmark, we support organizations in implementing QMS solutions that align with their size, scope, and complexity. Key aspects of a QMS Setup:

  • Regulatory compliance: Ensure conformity with applicable laws and standards, including ISO 13485:2016, 21 CFR 820, EU Regulations 2017/745 & 746, and MDSAP.

  • Tailored solutions: Processes adapted to your organization’s specific activities and goals.

  • Documentation: Development of all required procedures, policies, and manuals.

  • Stakeholder training: Educating teams to adopt and maintain QMS standards effectively.

  • Continuous improvement: Embedding practices that drive efficiency and reduce waste.

Why is a QMS important?

QMS implementation is not only mandatory for regulatory compliance but also brings significant business benefits:

  • Mandatory for certification: No QMS, no product certification or market access.

  • Operational consistency: Standardized processes lead to fewer errors and inefficiencies.

  • Continuous improvement: A QMS fosters an environment of ongoing optimization.

  • Enhanced decision-making: Evidence-based processes enable better business decisions.

  • Customer satisfaction: Improved product quality translates to higher patient and customer trust.
